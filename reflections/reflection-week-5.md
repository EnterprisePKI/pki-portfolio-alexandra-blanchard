Week 5 Reflection

What did you learn this week?
I learned that OCSP is a real-time reputation check. CRL is your scheduled recall list, but clients must be able to reach it.

What concept was most challenging?
Learning soft-fail is an availability and not a security feature. 

Where does this concept appear in real-world systems?
In a real-world setting, a PKI engineer may have to design revocation paths to be resilient enough that a hard-fail is safe.

How would you explain this topic to a non-technical audience?
The CSR field communicates to the CA when it says, “ Here is my public key, names I want bound to that key, and here is my proof I control the matching private key.”

What questions remain?
In a strict PKI, note how the HSM of your enterprise will produce an output to display when a CSR signature doesn’t verify its issuance?
